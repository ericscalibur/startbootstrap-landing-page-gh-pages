Initiatives

We like to get our hands dirty.

The “Living Soil Saves Lives”

program provides training for rural farmers in India’s suicide belt on the ‘soil food web’ and various composting techniques with the goal of assisting their transition back to natural, organic farming methods. Since 2011, THP has presented on the ‘Living Soil’ to over 2,500 individuals in 6 different states across India and has helped 12 farmer’s cooperatives by providing infrastructure such as water harvesting, irrigation and compost systems.

Cleveland Seed Bank

We are an alliance of self-reliant seed savers who collect, grow, and share open-pollinated seeds. We act as an independent source of locally adapted seeds that directly services Cleveland’s organic growers. We also present public educational programs on the genetic diversity of plant varieties and preservation of open-pollinated seeds.

Sustainable Schools

The Sustainable Schools Initiative in Kenya provides education and infrastructure to impoverished schools, allowing students and communities to become working models for sustainability. Our project will take the Sustainable School model, which we have been developing at Daraja Academy for the past 4 years, to more schools in the region. Setting Up Soil Labs

When farmers can see the microbes for themselves, the soil web takes on a whole new level of significance. Farmers practicing conventional farming are more likely to make the switch to organic if they can see first hand the difference in the soil biology of their soil compared to a biodiverse, living soil.
